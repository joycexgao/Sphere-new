Sphere

designing mobile interface for online corporate education platform

overview

Sphere is an online corporate education startup that aims to improve employees' functional skills by providing them with livestream courses instructed by field experts. In my UI/UX course, each team is tasked to iteratively design a piece of user experience for a startup's product, without seeing the design of the startup's actual product. With a passion for education technology, my team chose Sphere.

  • Project Type: course team project
  • Role: researcher, UX designer
  • Time: 3 weeks
  • Constraint: the design process was mostly set by the course assignment, but we added the research section and iterated the prototype more than required

Research - Why Livestream?

Livestream lessons facilitate learning by fostering an interactive learning enviornment.

However, our research also shows that students in similar professional education programs prefer recorded lessons for time flexibility. But due to the constraint of this project, we cannot discuss this product choice with Sphere. We decided to compromise by incorporating livestreams as well as a library of past recorded lessons.

more user feedback

After addressing the previous round of feedback, we submitted our mockup v2.0 to usertesting.com.

Unlike our peers, the users who tested our prototype on usertesting.com are all around 30 years of age, which is closer to the demographics of Sphere’s target audience. These users helped us locate problems that did not arise during the first round of user testing.

All users were confused by the lack of labels in the navigation bar to varying degrees The association between the star icon and "instructor" is unclear. For instance, one user thought it means “favorite” Users were confused by the lack of navigation from the livestream page to the course page

Overall, all users found the interface very easy to learn! They also found it natural to directly reach out to an instructor, which is important for Sphere's focus on building an interactive learning community.

conclusions & lessons

Feedback is important in making sure that every aspect of the design is intentional. In this project, peer and user feedback for the first two mockups picked out many design choices that were not thoroughly considered from a user perspective. Finding the right demographics of users is crucial! Because of the structure of the course assignment, our first round of user feedback came from tech-savvy students. For them, the unlabeled navigation bar does not hinder learnability, but it poses a difficulty for a slightly older user demographic. If users are expected to learn something, the instruction should be clear and obvious. Users did not pick up our attempt to establish an association between the star icon and “instructor”, because the signal is too subtle.

Things I would do differently

If this were a real project commissioned by Sphere, I would inquire more about the livestreaming concept. In our research, although we found evidence in support of livestream lessons, we also found evidence against it, the most important of which is time flexibility. Do user testing on people who more closely resemble the target users. Due to the design of this assignment, our first round of feedback came from our peers, who can have a pretty different perspective from our target users.
